About Maltese Language

Maltese (Maltese: Malti) is the national language of Malta and an official language of the European Union. It is a Semitic language derived from late medieval Sicilian Arabic with a large number of loanwords from Italian, French, and English.

Learning Maltese connects you to Malta's rich history and culture that spans thousands of years, from ancient Phoenician settlements to its modern European identity.

Quick Facts About Malta

  • Population: Approx. 500,000
  • Official Languages: Maltese & English
  • Location: Mediterranean Sea, south of Sicily
  • Known for: Beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and unique culture
